Executive Summary: AAS-based Digital Twin
Research Context

The energy transition is fundamentally transforming low-voltage (LV) distribution grids. With the rise of distributed energy resources (DERs) like solar PV and electric vehicles, Distribution System Operators (DSOs) face severe challenges in grid observability due to data fragmentation across siloed information systems.

Methodology & Innovation

This thesis addresses the interoperability gap by proposing a Digital Twin framework centered on the Asset Administration Shell (AAS), as specified by the IEC 63278 standard. The primary innovation is the implementation of the AAS as a Single Source of Truth (SSOT), consolidating static planning data, manufacturer information, and real-time operational measurements.

The research established semantic mappings from the Common Information Model (CIM) to the AAS metamodel, ensuring that grid data remains standards-compliant and discoverable.

Results & Validation
  • Structural Integrity: Successfully instantiated a hierarchical Digital Twin, mapping everything from system-wide topology to specific transformer oil volumes.
  • Numerical Accuracy: A 15-bus Distribution System State Estimation (DSSE) use case confirmed a Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) below 0.1%.
  • Enhanced Observability: The system successfully calculated voltages at unmetered nodes, providing visibility into previously "dark" parts of the network.

Thesis title: Definition and Development of a Low-Voltage Grid Digital Twin using the Asset Administration Shell as a Single-Source-of-Truth for Power System Data and Services.
